Designing for digital accessibility

Make your designs accessible, and share their beauty with everyone. Explore UI and interaction design principles that contribute to creating barrier-free experiences. This is great for visual and UX designers, and digital professionals.

What you’ll learn

  • improving readability using colour, layout and spacing
  • how responsive design supports UI customisation
  • accessible alternatives for media and images
  • structuring content for easy navigation
  • designing for keyboard, pointer and voice interaction
  • creating accessible forms.

Who should attend

  • UX designers
  • Visual designers
  • Digital professionals

Knowledge level

  • Basic knowledge of UI design is required.
  • No prior accessibility knowledge is needed

Course outline

1. Colour – 1 hour

  • Using colour
  • Contrast for text and non-text content
  • Activity: Testing for colour contrast (including on your own site)
  • Activity: Colour contrast quiz

2. Layout and proximity – 1 hour

  • Page regions
  • Browser and text zoom
  • Orientation
  • Customising text
  • Proximity
  • Activity: Experiencing browser zoom

3. Images and graphics – 1 hour

  • Using images effectively
  • Images and text alternatives

4. Multimedia and animation – 1 hour

  • Multimedia and media alternatives
  • Audio and video considerations
  • Animation and movement
  • Activity: Multimedia quiz

5. Navigation and content structure – 1 hour

  • Page regions
  • Page title and headings
  • Content structure
  • Links

6. Input methods: keyboard and voice – 1 hour

  • Keyboard navigation
  • Focus order and focus management

7. Input methods: touch – 1 hour

  • Content that appears on hover or focus
  • Simple pointer gestures
  • Target sizes

8. Forms and time limits – 1 hour

  • Placeholders and labels
  • Instructions
  • Error messages
  • Time limits
