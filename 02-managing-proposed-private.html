Managing accessibility in digital projects

Need some help plotting your course to create accessible digital products? In this course, we cover how accessibility decisions need to be factored into all aspects of planning, delivery and launch of your product.

What you‘ll learn

  • Impacts of accessibility on users‘ experience
  • Legal requirements for your organisation
  • Guidelines and standards including the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) and procurement
  • Incorporating accessibility throughout the project lifecycle
  • Technology choices
  • Communications and ongoing maintenance

Who should attend

  • Project Managers
  • Business Analysts
  • Product Owners
  • Digital Professionals

Knowledge level

No prior accessibility knowledge is needed.

Course outline

Session 1

This session will explore the basics of accessibility including legal, reputation and commercial reasons. There will be a discussion about all the different types of people who could interact with your digital products, and some of the assistive technologies they may use. The session will also explore disability user profiles and how they can be used to improve all the phases of your project life-cycle.

  • Why should you care about accessibility?
  • Let‘s meet some people
  • Accessibility user profiles
  • Activity: Creating accessibility user profiles
  • Activity: Where could these user profiles be used?

Session 2

This session will explore the basics of accessibility including legal, reputation and commercial reasons. There will be an overview of WCAG, what it is, why it is important and how it is structured. The session will then look at a wide range of accessibility testing tools. There will be an activity where participants can use the Axe tool to a selection of sites and understand how to use this tool in real-world environments. The session will conclude by outlining ways that teams can measure the importance of accessibility issues and prioritise them for remediation.

  • What is WCAG?
  • Accessibility testing tools
  • Activity: Automated testing with Axe
  • Measuring the importance of issues
  • Prioritising how issues with be addressed
  • Discussion: How do you prioritise?
  • Activity: How accessibility-mature is your team?
  • Activity: Who is responsible?
